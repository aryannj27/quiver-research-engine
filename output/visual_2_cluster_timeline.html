Cluster Timeline

At-a-glance visualization of congressional trading clusters — spot the moments when multiple members converge on a ticker.

The Insight

Congressional trades become newsworthy when they move together.

One senator buying NVDA is noise. Five senators buying NVDA in the same week is a story. The current Quiver product shows trades as a table — chronological, one per row. That format hides clusters. A calendar-style timeline makes them unmissable.

In production, this becomes a Chart.js / D3 scatter plot with live Quiver API data, hover tooltips showing member name + disclosure link, and a filter bar (House/Senate, Party, direction, dollar threshold). Rendering time <200ms for 30-day lookback on full congress data.
The Design — Why This Layout

Horizontal time axis, tickers stacked vertically. Each row is a ticker. Each dot is one trade. Dots colored by direction (green/red), sized by dollar range. When dots bunch up within a 14-day window on the same row with the same color, the CLUSTER badge lights up. That's the anomaly.

A single chart replaces what is currently 50+ rows of tabular data. Pattern recognition happens pre-cognitively.

The "So What" — if a retail investor sees this at 7 AM Tuesday

They scroll the chart. Their eye catches the orange CLUSTER badge on MSFT. They see three green dots bunched together, one noticeably bigger. They read the label: "Gottheimer $500K+". They now know — without reading a single sentence of text — that three members of Congress just converged on Microsoft in a single week, with one of them making an unusually large leveraged bet. That is the entire story, delivered in one image. The newsletter then provides the context.
